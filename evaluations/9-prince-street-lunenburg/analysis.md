## 🔴 Critical Issues — Resolve Before Making an Offer

These three items can each independently kill or fundamentally change the deal. Do not commit $35K-$110K until all three are resolved.

### 1. CMHC Insurability for 1761 Stone Foundation

**Risk:** The entire 5% down scenario depends on CMHC mortgage insurance. CMHC can refuse to insure properties that don't meet their appraisal standards. A 265-year-old building with a stone foundation could trigger:
- Additional conditions (structural engineer report required)
- Lower appraisal than purchase price → bigger down payment needed
- Outright refusal to insure

**If CMHC declines:** The 5% down scenario evaporates. You'd need 20% down ($110,125 cash to close) — 2.8× the capital requirement.

**Action:** Get a mortgage broker assessment of CMHC eligibility for this specific property before making an offer.

### 2. Insurance Quotes (3+ Required)

**Risk:** The original model used $2,400/yr for insurance. This is almost certainly too low. Heritage multi-unit properties with STR operations face four premium multipliers:
1. Heritage building (1761) — replacement cost for heritage-compliant materials is 2-5× standard
2. Multi-unit duplex — higher liability
3. STR operation — standard homeowner's insurance won't cover it; need commercial or STR rider (+30-50%)
4. Stone foundation — some insurers refuse coverage entirely

**Realistic range:** $3,500-$6,000/yr. Some Lunenburg heritage property owners report being dropped after claims or only finding coverage through specialty insurers at 2-3× standard rates.

**If insurance is $7,000+/yr:** Adds $383+/mo. Combined with other costs, the deal becomes unattractive.

**Action:** Get minimum 3 insurance quotes before going firm. Ask specifically about heritage replacement cost basis, STR coverage, and stone foundation acceptability.

### 3. Heritage Structural Engineer Assessment

**Risk:** A 265-year-old stone foundation is the single biggest unknown. Failure modes and costs:

| Failure Mode | Likelihood | Cost to Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Mortar deterioration (repointing) | HIGH | $5,000-$20,000 |
| Moisture penetration | HIGH | $3,000-$15,000 |
| Foundation wall bowing | Medium | $15,000-$50,000 |
| Settlement/sinking | Low-Medium | $20,000-$80,000 |
| Stone spalling (freeze-thaw) | Medium | $5,000-$25,000 |

**Positive signal:** The building has survived 265 years. If the foundation were fundamentally failing, signs would have appeared.

**Action:** Heritage-specialist structural engineer assessment ($2,000-$3,000) before closing. Not a regular home inspector — someone experienced with pre-1800 Maritime stone foundations.

## 10. Tax Considerations (Canadian Context)

### If Owner-Occupying 2-Bed, Studio as Rental

- Studio rental income reported on **T776** (Statement of Real Estate Rentals)
- Deductible expenses (proportional to rental portion, ~35-40% of building):
  - Mortgage interest (not principal)
  - Property tax (proportional)
  - Insurance (proportional)
  - Utilities (proportional)
  - Maintenance/repairs (proportional)
  - CCA (capital cost allowance) — optional but reduces cost basis on sale
- At Graham's marginal rate (NS combined ~40-45%), a $5,000 tax deduction saves ~$2,000-$2,250

### Principal Residence Exemption

- The 2-bed unit qualifies for **principal residence exemption** on sale
- The studio/rental portion does NOT — capital gains tax applies on the rental share
- Rough split: 60% exempt (2-bed), 40% taxable (studio)
- On $77K of appreciation over 5 years: ~$31K taxable, ~$15.5K capital gain, ~$7K in tax
- **Factor ~$5,000-$10,000 in tax liability on the rental portion at sale**

### STR Tax Treatment

- Airbnb income is rental income (not business income) if you don't provide hotel-like services
- GST/HST registration required if gross STR revenue exceeds $30,000/yr — studio alone is under this threshold at current occupancy
- If both units as STR, you'd likely exceed $30K and need to register + charge HST (15% in NS)
