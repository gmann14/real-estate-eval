# Nova Scotia — Defaults & Rules

> All public data. Verify against source links before final offer. Rates as of
> 2026-04-17; update when the province/municipalities change them.

## Land/Deed Transfer Tax

Nova Scotia uses municipal **Deed Transfer Tax (DTT)**, set by each municipality.

| Municipality | DTT Rate | Notes |
|--------------|----------|-------|
| Halifax Regional Municipality | 1.5% | |
| Town of Lunenburg | 1.5% | |
| Municipality of Chester | 1.5% | |
| Mahone Bay | 1.5% | |
| Bridgewater | 1.5% | |
| Wolfville | 1.5% | |
| Truro | 1.5% | |
| Default (most NS munis) | 1.5% | |
| Exceptions | verify | A few municipalities have different rates — always confirm |

**Non-resident deed transfer tax (NRDTT):** An additional **5%** on the purchase
price applies to buyers who are not Nova Scotia residents (with certain
exemptions, including intent to relocate within 6 months). This is on top of
municipal DTT.

- Source: https://beta.novascotia.ca/apply-land-transfer-tax-refund

## Property Tax

Set by each municipality. Residential rate expressed per $100 of assessed
value. Assessed values are set by **PVSC** (Property Valuation Services
Corporation) and typically run 75–85% of market value.

| Municipality | Residential Rate (per $100) | Year |
|--------------|----------------------------|------|
| Town of Lunenburg | 1.376 | 2024 |
| Halifax (urban) | ~1.253 | 2024 |
| Halifax (suburban) | ~1.103 | 2024 |
| Halifax (rural) | ~0.930 | 2024 |
| Bridgewater | ~1.380 | 2024 |
| Mahone Bay | ~1.280 | 2024 |

> **Always verify** current-year rate on the municipal website — these change
> annually at budget time.

**Capped Assessment Program (CAP):** Caps year-over-year assessment increases
for owner-occupied residential properties. CAP does not transfer on sale — the
new owner starts fresh at market-based assessment. Factor this in for
purchase-year property tax.

## Non-Resident Property Tax

Nova Scotia repealed the 2022 non-resident property tax in 2023 — no ongoing
annual surtax. The NRDTT on purchase still applies.

## STR Regulation

### Provincial

- **Tourist Accommodations Registration Act**: all STRs (Airbnb, VRBO,
  short-term rentals under 28 nights) must register with Tourism Nova Scotia.
  Annual fee: $50 (2024 rate).
- Platforms (Airbnb/VRBO) are required to display the registration number and
  cannot list unregistered properties.

### Municipal overlays

- **Halifax**: STRs in residential zones restricted to the host's primary
  residence (as of 2023 bylaw). Commercial zones permit non-primary STRs.
- **Lunenburg**: No STR-specific bylaw as of 2026-04; STRs are permitted in
  residential zones. **Heritage district exterior changes require approval
  from the Lunenburg Heritage Advisory Committee.**
- **Mahone Bay**: Similar to Lunenburg. Verify current bylaws.

> Check the municipal website for current STR rules before offering — these
> change frequently in tourist markets.

## Rent Control / Tenant Law

- **Residential Tenancies Act (NS)** — landlord-friendly relative to ON/BC.
- No ongoing rent cap since 2024 (prior 2% cap lifted). Rent increases limited
  to once per 12 months.
- N-series notices (lease termination for owner use) are available but require
  defined grounds.

## First-Time Buyer Programs

- **Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)** — federal, up to $60,000 from RRSP (2024 limit,
  subject to change). Non-NS-specific but applicable.
- **First Home Savings Account (FHSA)** — federal.
- **NS First-Time Home Buyers' Rebate** — rebate on deed transfer tax for
  first-time buyers of newly built homes (limited scope; check eligibility).

## Tax Treatment (Rental Income — Federal + NS combined)

- Rental income: **T776 — Statement of Real Estate Rentals** (federal form,
  reported on personal return).
- Combined marginal rates (federal + NS, 2025 brackets):
  | Taxable income | Combined marginal rate |
  |----------------|----------------------|
  | Up to $59,000 | ~30% |
  | $59,001–$93,000 | ~37% |
  | $93,001–$150,000 | ~43% |
  | $150,001–$250,000 | ~47% |
  | Over $250,000 | ~54% |

## HST

- **15% HST** applies in NS (federal GST + provincial). STR operators must
  register and charge HST if gross revenue exceeds **$30,000/year** (small
  supplier threshold).

## CMHC / Insured Mortgage Context in NS

- Lunenburg, Mahone Bay, Chester: common-enough CMHC-insured market. No NS-
  specific CMHC restrictions beyond federal rules.
- **Heritage buildings and stone foundations**: CMHC insurers may require
  additional documentation or refuse insurance. Always get a broker
  pre-assessment before relying on high-ratio financing for pre-1900
  buildings.

## Insurance Considerations

- Heritage multi-unit properties with STR operations in NS typically face
  insurance quotes in the **$3,500–$6,000/yr** range.
- Stone-foundation properties: some insurers refuse coverage; specialty
  insurers (e.g., Pacific Marine Underwriting, Aviva Heritage) may quote
  higher. Get 3+ quotes.
