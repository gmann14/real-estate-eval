# Halifax Regional Municipality — Municipal Rules

> Halifax Regional Municipality (HRM) covers urban Halifax, Dartmouth,
> Bedford, Sackville, and a large rural fringe. Rules vary by tax
> zone and by the governing land-use bylaw. This file captures the
> common-case baselines.

## Deed Transfer Tax

**1.5%** of purchase price — standard NS rate. Provincial NRDTT (5%)
applies to non-NS-resident buyers; see `config/provinces/ns.md`.

## Property tax (residential, per $100 assessed value)

Halifax splits into three service levels. The rate you actually pay
depends on the property's tax zone:

| Zone                  | Residential rate | Year |
|-----------------------|------------------|------|
| Urban (full service)  | ~1.253           | 2024 |
| Suburban              | ~1.103           | 2024 |
| Rural                 | ~0.930           | 2024 |

**Capped Assessment Program (CAP):** Caps year-over-year assessment
increases for owner-occupied residential properties. CAP does not
transfer on sale — the new owner starts fresh at market-based
assessment. Factor this in for purchase-year property tax.

**Verify the current-year rate** on the HRM website before a final
offer.

## Short-term rentals (STR)

Halifax's STR bylaw (adopted 2023, iterated since):

- STR of a **principal residence** is generally permitted in residential
  zones with a provincial registration.
- STR of a **non-principal residence** (pure-investment STR) is
  restricted to commercial/mixed-use zones in most of HRM.
- Enforcement has been increasing. Confirm the property's zoning allows
  commercial STR before modeling a pure-STR scenario for a non-primary
  unit.

## ADU / secondary suite rules

- HRM broadly permits secondary suites in most residential zones
  (internal basement/attic suite or an addition).
- Detached backyard suites (garden suites / ADUs) allowed in more zones
  after recent bylaw updates; setback and lot-coverage rules apply.
- Suite must meet building code: fire separation, independent egress,
  ceiling height, sound insulation.

## Zoning categories that matter for multi-unit

- **R-1 / R-2** — single and two-family residential (duplex often
  permitted in R-2)
- **ER-1 / ER-2** — established residential (rowhouses, small multis)
- **HR / HR-1** — high-density residential (triplex + apartment)
- **Centre Plan zones** (regional centre urban area) — COR, HR, ER,
  DOWN; check Centre Plan maps for the parcel
- **Mixed-use corridors** — residential over commercial common

HRM's Centre Plan is the operative land-use document inside the
regional centre; outside it, older land-use bylaws apply. Always pull
the zoning confirmation for the parcel before offer.

## Known gotchas

- Heritage registers (provincial and municipal) can constrain exterior
  modifications, especially in the Downtown Halifax and Barrington
  Street areas.
- Flood plain overlays along the harbour and inner streams.
- Older housing stock (pre-1980) frequently has knob-and-tube or
  aluminum wiring — plan on an electrical inspection.
