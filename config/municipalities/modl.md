# Lunenburg South Shore — Municipal Rules

> Covers the south-shore cluster around the Municipality of the District of
> Lunenburg (MODL): MODL itself, Town of Lunenburg, Town of Mahone Bay,
> Town of Bridgewater, and the neighbouring Municipality of the District
> of Chester. They're governed separately but share most rules; this file
> captures the shared baseline and flags differences.

## Jurisdictions covered

- MODL (Municipality of the District of Lunenburg)
- Town of Lunenburg
- Town of Mahone Bay
- Town of Bridgewater
- Municipality of the District of Chester

## Deed Transfer Tax

Standard NS municipal DTT: **1.5%** of purchase price. No local exceptions
in this cluster as of 2026.

Provincial NRDTT (5%) applies to non-NS-resident buyers; see
`config/provinces/ns.md`.

## Short-term rentals (STR)

- Nova Scotia requires provincial tourist accommodation registration for
  any STR of a full unit (Registry of Tourist Accommodations).
- MODL and most towns in this cluster currently allow STR in residential
  zones subject to provincial registration.
- **Town of Lunenburg** has been tightening: principal-residence-only
  rules have been discussed; confirm current status on the town's
  planning page before acquiring a pure-STR unit there.
- Mahone Bay has seen community pushback — check current bylaws.

## ADU / secondary suite rules

- NS provincial building code permits secondary suites in most
  single-family zones subject to building code (fire separation, egress,
  ceiling height, sound insulation).
- Backyard suites (detached ADUs / garden suites) generally require
  municipal approval; lot size and setback rules vary.
- Rural MODL and District of Chester are typically more permissive than
  the towns.

## Zoning categories that matter for multi-unit

- **R1 / R2** — single-family / two-family residential; duplex often
  permitted in R2
- **R3 / MR** — multi-residential; triplex / 4+ units
- **CD / Comprehensive Development** — site-specific; check the
  registered development agreement
- **Mixed Use / Downtown / Village** — often allow residential above
  commercial

Always pull the zoning confirmation letter from the relevant planning
department before writing an offer on a multi-unit.

## Known gotchas

- Waterfront lots: confirm septic setback and coastal-protection rules
  (NS Coastal Protection Act — still being finalized; check latest).
- Flood risk mapping is available from NS Open Data; low-lying
  south-shore lots have real exposure.
- Well + septic: standard outside the towns. Factor $500–1,500 for
  pre-purchase water and septic inspections.
