# CMHC Mortgage Insurance — Reference

> Public CMHC tables. Verify at https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/professionals/
> project-funding-and-mortgage-financing/mortgage-loan-insurance before final
> offer. Last updated 2026-04-17.

## Minimum Down Payment (owner-occupied)

| Purchase Price | Minimum Down Payment |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Up to $500,000 | 5% |
| $500,001 – $1,499,999 | 5% on first $500K + 10% on portion above |
| $1,500,000+ | 20% (uninsured) |

> Dec 2024 rule change raised the insurable cap from $1M to $1.5M.

## Standard Premium Rates (insured, owner-occupied)

Premium = `premium_rate × mortgage_after_down_payment`, added to mortgage principal.

| Loan-to-Value (LTV) | Down Payment | Premium Rate |
|--------------------|--------------|--------------|
| 65% or less | 35%+ | 0.60% |
| 65.01% – 75% | 25–35% | 1.70% |
| 75.01% – 80% | 20–25% | 2.40% |
| 80.01% – 85% | 15–20% | 2.80% |
| 85.01% – 90% | 10–15% | 3.10% |
| 90.01% – 95% | 5–10% | 4.00% |

### Surcharges

| Situation | Surcharge |
|-----------|-----------|
| Extended amortization (30yr insured, as of Aug 2024) | +0.20% |
| Non-traditional down payment (e.g., borrowed) | +0.15% |
| Blended amortization / second home | +0.20% |

## Owner-Occupancy Rule

CMHC insurance is **only available for owner-occupied** properties. Non-owner-
occupied (pure investment) requires 20% down minimum and cannot use CMHC.

### Multi-Unit Owner-Occupied

| Property Type | Max LTV (owner-occupied) |
|---------------|-------------------------|
| Single-family | 95% (5% down) |
| Duplex (2 units) | 95% (5% down) |
| Triplex (3 units) | 90% (10% down) |
| Fourplex (4 units) | 90% (10% down) |
| 5+ units | Multi-unit (commercial) insurance, different rules |

Owner must occupy at least one unit as principal residence.

## Amortization

| Situation | Max Amortization |
|-----------|------------------|
| CMHC-insured (standard) | 25 years |
| CMHC-insured (first-time + new build, Aug 2024+) | 30 years (+0.20% premium) |
| Uninsured (20%+ down) | 30 years |

## Other CMHC-Insured Lenders

Sagen (formerly Genworth) and Canada Guaranty offer equivalent insurance at
the same rates and with similar rules. Use whichever your lender prefers.

## CMHC Discretion / Refusal

CMHC can refuse insurance if:
- Property fails standard appraisal (age, condition, unusual features)
- Stone/rubble foundations may trigger scrutiny on pre-1900 buildings
- Leased land, shared driveways without easements, zoning issues
- Remote locations with thin comps

**Practical implication:** For heritage / pre-1900 / unusual properties, get a
broker to confirm CMHC eligibility *before* writing the offer. An appraisal
below offer price can require you to either increase your down payment or
walk.

## Calculation Example

**$485,000 purchase, 5% down:**
- Down payment: $485,000 × 5% = $24,250
- Base mortgage: $485,000 − $24,250 = $460,750
- LTV: 460,750 / 485,000 = 95.00% → premium rate 4.00%
- Premium: $460,750 × 4.00% = $18,430
- **Total mortgage: $460,750 + $18,430 = $479,180**

**Quebec, Ontario, Saskatchewan, Manitoba:** Premium is a taxable supply (PST
applies to the premium in those provinces and must be paid in cash at closing,
not added to the mortgage).
