# Disclaimer

This tool produces financial modeling for residential real estate purchases.
Every number in every output is:

- **Not financial advice.** It is a decision aid. Do not rely on it as the
  sole basis for a purchase decision.
- **Not legal advice.** STR bylaws, tenancy law, zoning, and heritage
  restrictions change. Always confirm with the municipality and, where
  material, a real estate lawyer.
- **Not tax advice.** Canadian tax rules around rental income, principal
  residence exemptions, CCA, HST, and capital gains are complex and change
  frequently. Consult a CPA or tax professional before relying on any tax
  calculation in this tool.
- **Not a mortgage pre-approval.** CMHC insurability, rate availability, and
  qualification are determined by lenders and insurers, not by this tool. Get
  a broker pre-approval before making firm offers on properties that depend on
  insured financing.
- **Not a property inspection.** This tool cannot assess the physical
  condition of a building. Hire a licensed inspector. For heritage, pre-1900,
  or unusual buildings, hire a specialist structural engineer.

## Known limitations

- Revenue estimates for LTR and STR are based on comparable listings and
  historical data. Actual results can vary by 30%+ based on execution, market
  conditions, seasonality, platform changes, and regulatory changes.
- Appreciation assumptions are deliberately conservative. They reflect
  long-run averages, not short-run market dynamics. Real appreciation in any
  given 5–10 year window can vary widely.
- The AI agent may make errors when gathering data from listings or
  municipal websites. **Verify every number that drives a material decision.**
- Province and municipality data files reflect rules as of the `Last updated`
  date in each file. They may be out of date.

## Use at your own risk

By using this tool, you acknowledge that any real estate purchase decision is
solely your own, and that neither the author nor contributors are responsible
for financial losses, opportunities missed, or any other outcome from your
decisions.

Always consult:

- A licensed **mortgage broker** before committing to financing
- A **real estate lawyer** before committing to a purchase
- A **CPA or tax professional** before relying on tax projections
- A **licensed home inspector** before closing
- A **specialist structural engineer** for heritage or unusual buildings
- An **insurance broker** to confirm quotes before going firm
